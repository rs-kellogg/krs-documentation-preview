## Description

The Philadelphia Stock Exchange, founded in 1790, is the oldest organized stock exchange in the nation. As one of North America's primary marketplaces for the trading of stocks, equity options, index options, and currency options, the PHLX has been a market leader in the development and introduction of innovative products and services.

The PHLX trades more than 2,800 stocks, 740 equity options, 12 sector index options, and 100 currency pairs. On the equity floor, PHLX's PACE (Philadelphia Automated Communication and Execution) system was one of the first automated equity trading systems on any exchange.

Notable indices and products include:

- **Semiconductor Sector (SOX) Index Option** — one of the most actively traded sector index options and a widely quoted benchmark of the high-tech industry
- **Oil Service Sector (OSX)** — the industry's most successful new sector index option
- **Gold/Silver Sector (XAU)**, **KBW Bank Sector (BKX)**, and **Utility Sector (UTY)** — established industry indicators

The PHLX also created the **United Currency Options Market (UCOM)**, providing both standardized and customized currency options across 100 currency pairs. Customized options offer choice of expiration date, strike price, premium payment, and any combination of 10 available currencies.
