## Description

Datastream provides an extensive historical financial database offering global coverage across multiple asset classes and economic data:

- **Commodities** — Explore more than 140,000 commodity series, with coverage from 183 sources and exchanges including Goldman Sachs, AIG and CRB.
- **Economics** — Datastream Economics integrates in-depth economic coverage, consistent data history and critical timeliness on one platform with unparalleled financial market data. The database provides comprehensive coverage of national economies, from the market moving indicators to fully detailed economic and financial accounts.
- **Equities** — Historical data for over 103,000 active equities plus over 166,000 inactive equities covering 100 developed and emerging markets with over 200 fields including prices, volumes, market capitalization, earnings, dividend and corporate action data. For many markets, there is full coverage of all traded equity instruments with over 50 years of history for the key developed markets. Data is sourced direct from exchanges, leading international and local suppliers, and published report.
- **Futures** — Datastream provides extensive coverage of end-of-day historical futures. Users have access to all available pricing and trading data for both active and inactive futures. In addition, 34,000 continuous series determined with six methods of calculation exist for various term structures.

Datastream draws on national statistical agencies, central banks, international organizations, consulting companies, and research institutes. Depending on the type of series, data is available on a daily, weekly, monthly, or annual basis.

## Access

This dataset is available via [Wharton Research Data Services (WRDS)](https://wrds-www.wharton.upenn.edu/), under the [LSEG](https://wrds-www.wharton.upenn.edu/pages/get-data/lseg/) subscription. WRDS offers both a web interface for small interactive queries and the WRDS Cloud for running batch programs. Faculty may request a temporary class account for course use. Datastream on WRDS is organized into four separate products: Equities, Economics, Commodities, and Futures.
